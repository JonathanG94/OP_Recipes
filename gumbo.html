Jonathan's Gumbo

Description

This is a gumbo that I've developed over the years, it's super simple, quick to make and ideal for feeding a diverse set of guests as it works on the basis of establishing a gravy and adding "toppings"

Toppings can be the standard chicken and sausage, to non-standard - such as aubergine or mushroom if you're serving to vegans or vegetarians.

I like to operate on a "hot and quick" basis to creating the roux, and use Geesowska Kielbasa to substitute for Andouille - give it a go!

Ingredients

Makes approximately 6-8 portions

  • 100g vegetable oil
  • 120g plain flour
  • 2 onions
  • 6 small bell peppers
  • 4 sticks of celery
  • 1 tbsp minced garlic
  • 2 small carrots
  • 1 tsp cayenne pepper
  • 2 tbsp Cajun seasoning (spicy)
  • 2 tsp Crystal hot sauce (or your favourite vinegar based hot sauce)
  • deglazing liquid - such as whisky or beer
  • 1 litre veg/chicken stock
  • Toppings - such as chicken, andouille, smoky mushrooms

Steps

  1. Rough dice pepper, onion and celery, finely mince garlic and carrot
  2. Add oil to pan, get nice and hot, add flour and immediately begin whisking. Keep it on a medium-high heat and whisk continuously. Keep going to chocolatey colour and a nice nutty aroma - about 10 mins if hot enough
  3. Add onion, pepper and celery (trinity) and carrot to pan and sautee until tender-ish, Add garlic, cayenne and cajun seasoning to bloom spices
  4. Deglaze, add stock (slowly while mixing) and finally add “toppings”
  5. Simmer for at least an hour but ideally two to get rid of the raw flour taste and develop flavour
  6. Serve with white rice and garnish with thinly sliced spring onion
