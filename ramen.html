Jonathan's Ramen

Description

Ramen is a dish that home chefs tend to miss out on due to an "all or nothing " mindset; it's either an 18 hour slow simmered tonkotsu or a packet of instant ramen. What if I told you that there's a perfect middle ground?

This ramen recipe provides an effective template for combining a simple, but surprisingly tasty broth with homemade Ajitama egg, chashu pork, miso tare and sauteed spinach. These aren't contained within the scope of this recipe but I like to use the equilibrium method for the egg and a pressure cooker for the pork.

The end result is a ramen that punches well above its weight in terms of flavour while retaining a home-cooked charm without leaving your hob on overnight! Give this a go and experiment with your own preferences.

Ingredients

Makes one bowl of ramen - can be scaled for more

  • 400 ml hot water
  • 1 zero salt stock cube (chicken or veg)
  • 3 slices of chashu pork
  • 1 ajitama egg
  • 1 finger sized "log" of sauteed spinach
  • 80g ramen noodles
  • 4 tbsp miso tare
  • (optional) flavouring oil - such as chilli oil

Steps

  1. Prepare ramen noodles according to the packet instructions
  2. Combine boiling water, stock cube and miso tare - note that this can be done in the bowl or in the wok - both of which give different results in terms of presentation and flavour
  3. Once noodles are cooked, strain off water and shake forcefully to dry as much as possible
  4. Add noodles to bowl with broth, lift with chopsticks and lay down to create a "bed" for the toppings
  5. Add chashu pork, sliced ajitama egg and sauteed spinach
  6. Add flavouring oil over the top and serve immediately
